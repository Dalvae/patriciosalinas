# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Four audiences, in priority order, across three languages (English, Spanish, Swedish):

1. **Curators & galleries** — art-world professionals evaluating the work for exhibitions, editions, or representation. They arrive deliberate and skeptical; they judge the site as evidence of seriousness.
2. **Press & editors** — journalists and photo editors looking for context, bio, and press material quickly.
3. **Collectors & buyers** — people considering prints or editions (e.g. Björkö Project Editions); they need confidence in provenance and quality, never a shopping-cart experience.
4. **General public** — readers discovering the visual essays for their own sake; they should be able to sink into the work without friction.

## Product Purpose

The portfolio of Patricio Salinas, a professional photographer whose visual essays explore existential themes, memory, and identity — projects include *Atacama* and *Walter Benjamin*. The site is the canonical reference point for his work: it must make a curator take the work seriously within seconds, give press what they need, and let everyone else experience the essays as essays. Success: the photographs and texts are encountered at museum quality, and inquiries (exhibition, press, edition) follow.

## Brand Personality

**Literary, austere, contemplative.**

- **Literary intellect** — essayistic and referential (Walter Benjamin is a subject, not just a title). The site should read like a thoughtful publication where text and image are equals.
- **Austere elegance** — refined restraint: fine serif typography (Playfair Display, Abril Fatface), near-black ink, a single gold accent, black-and-white photography. Gallery-print feel.
- **Contemplative gravity** — museum-like quiet. The visitor slows down; nothing on the page hurries them.

## References

- **manololaguillo.com** — deliberately understated structure; plain direct navigation (Proyectos / CV / Contacto); the work takes absolute precedence over web design.
- **alexeytitarenko.com** — austere, documentary-focused; projects presented as long-arc series with years ("St. Petersburg 1991–2009"), conveying depth of engagement rather than individual images.
- **coopergorfer.com** — contemplative refinement through whitespace; consistent project-card rhythm; extended timelines signal conceptual seriousness over commercial production.

The common thread: photographer-first sites where restraint is confidence, projects read as bodies of work spanning years, and the design is nearly invisible.

## Anti-references

- **Stock photographer templates** (Squarespace/Format): full-bleed slideshows, script logos, "book a session" energy.
- **Trendy agency portfolios** (Awwwards-style): WebGL, cursor effects, scroll-jacking — anything that upstages the photographs.
- **Commercial/e-commerce feel**: prices, carts, promotional badges anywhere near the work.
- **Cold tech minimalism** (Stripe/Linear-style): minimal but corporate sans-serif sterility, with no literary warmth.

## Design Principles

1. **The photograph is the interface.** Every design decision defers to the work. Chrome, motion, and color exist only to frame black-and-white photography; if an element competes with an image, the element loses.
2. **Essay, not slideshow.** Text and image are equals. Projects are bodies of work with literary pacing — context, sequence, and captions matter as much as the pictures.
3. **Museum pace.** No urgency mechanics, no attention-grabbing animation. Generous whitespace and slow rhythm invite the visitor to linger.
4. **Restraint is confidence.** Like the reference sites, near-invisible design signals seriousness. One gold accent used sparingly outranks any decorative system.
5. **Three languages, one voice.** Every layout must hold its composure across English, Spanish, and Swedish text lengths; the experience is identical in gravity regardless of language.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

No formal WCAG audit target, but follow strong defaults: readable contrast for body text, keyboard-reachable navigation and lightbox, `prefers-reduced-motion` alternatives for any animation (e.g. the infinite image loopers), and meaningful alt text for photographs in all three languages.
